About

Engineer, builder,
systems thinker.

I'm an automation and controls engineer who thrives at the intersection of hardware, software, and manufacturing. I've spent my career at early-stage hardware startups — walking into blank factory floors and building production systems from the ground up. No playbook, no precedent, no one to hand it off to. Just the problem and the drive to solve it.

My foundation is electrical engineering from Arizona State University, but I work across the full technical stack — PLC architecture, SCADA/HMI, mechanical design in CAD, and the manufacturing software that ties it all together. Fast-paced, interdisciplinary environments are where I do my best work. Six years mentoring a FIRST Robotics team sharpened how I break down complexity and communicate across disciplines. The rest I've learned by building things and refusing to stop until they work.

Outside the factory, I'm usually building something — personal robotics projects, hardware experiments, or recharging in the mountains. I have strong opinions about cooking.
